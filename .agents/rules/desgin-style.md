# Design Language Prompt: CURA - Trustworthy AI NGO Management

1. Brand Identity & Purpose: CURA is an autonomous AI management system for orphanages designed to bridge the trust gap between donors and NGOs. The design must convey professional reliability, financial transparency, and modern efficiency.

2. Core Color Palette: * **Primary Action (Cura Blue):** `#17439B` — Used for buttons, active states, and critical branding elements.

* 
**Background / Sidebar (Cura Dark):** `#1E1E1E` — A deep charcoal used for the navigation rail and high-contrast containers.


* 
**Secondary Text (Cura Grey):** `#83868E` — Used for labels, timestamps, and secondary information.


* 
**Surface / Cards:** `#FFFFFF` with a subtle `#F9FAFB` (gray-50) background for the main content area.



3. Typography: * **Primary Font:** **Jost**.

* **Scale:** Use a bold, clean hierarchy. Headers should be prominent (2xl or larger) to ensure readability on mobile devices and tablets.



4. UI/UX Principles: * **Mobile-First Orchestration:** The interface is optimized for staff on the go, utilizing single-column layouts for critical actions like the **Approval Queue**.

* 
**Rounded Geometry:** All containers, cards, and buttons use a high corner radius (`rounded-2xl` or `rounded-xl`) to feel approachable and modern.


* 
**Elevation:** Use light shadows (`shadow-sm`) and thin borders (`border-gray-100`) to create a layered "Command Center" feel without clutter.



5. Key Component Visuals: * **Dark Navigation Sidebar:** A narrow, high-contrast vertical rail with clean icons and a signature blue active indicator.

* 
**Actionable AI Cards:** Statistics cards that highlight trends (e.g., green +12%) and dedicated feeds for "AI Agent Activity" using colored indicator dots.


* 
**Transparent Ledger Feed:** A timeline-style list with distinct side-borders (border-l-4) in Cura Blue to emphasize donor impact.


* 
**One-Click Approval Interface:** High-contrast "Approve & Pay" buttons paired with an "AI Score" badge to facilitate rapid management sign-offs.



6. The "Vibe": The system should look and feel like a high-end fintech application tailored for social impact—less like a traditional database and more like an active, "vibe-coded" assistant that takes action on behalf of the community.
